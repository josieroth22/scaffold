## Financial Aid Strategy

**The home equity conversation, because you asked for the truth.** Every private school on this list requires the CSS Profile, and the CSS Profile asks about home equity. Your paid-off Boca Raton house, roughly $900K, is invisible to the FAFSA but visible to Yale, Brown, Harvard, Duke, Vanderbilt, WashU, USC, and Miami. Schools treat it differently, some cap it at a multiple of income, some count it fully, which is why the same family gets aid offers $20,000 apart from schools with identical sticker prices. At $200,000 income plus meaningful home equity, plan for need-based aid at the elite privates to land between "modest" and "a polite zero." The Net Price Calculators, run with your real numbers, are the only honest answer, and they are this month's homework, not April's.

**The FAFSA still matters.** File it in October when it opens, using your 2025 return, even though federal need aid is unlikely. It unlocks unsubsidized federal loans if you ever want them, it's required for Bright Futures disbursement mechanics, and one year from now your household has a second tuition on the horizon: her brother starts college the year Jocelyn finishes, and while they overlap only one year, that overlap year improves her aid math at need-based schools for her senior year. A small gift from the sibling gods; take it.

**Florida programs, by name.** Jocelyn already meets the **Bright Futures Florida Academic Scholars** thresholds: 3.5+ weighted core GPA (she has a 4.95), 1330+ SAT (she has a 1540), and the service/work hours requirement, which her camp summer and four years of BBYO satisfy several times over, but the hours must be *documented and submitted*, so have her log them with her counselor in August, not discover the gap in March. She must file the Florida Financial Aid Application (FFAA); calendar it now. FAS pays 100% of tuition and fees at Florida publics plus $300 per semester.

**The National Merit machine.** Semifinalist is a title; Finalist is money. The advancement application, confirming score (done), essay, and school endorsement, runs through her counselor with an early-October deadline. Roughly 15 in 16 Semifinalists advance. Once she's a Finalist: FSU's full ride attaches automatically, USC's $28,000+ requires the first-choice designation in spring, Vanderbilt's $2,000-5,000 requires the same, and you can only designate one school, so that decision waits until acceptances are in hand. This is the highest return-on-effort item in the entire plan: perhaps four hours of work protecting six figures of contingent value.

**What we're deliberately not doing:** Early Decision anywhere. ED would lift her odds at Vanderbilt or WashU meaningfully, but it binds before comparing offers, and your family's whole question is comparative: what does the dream cost against what the merit schools pay? You cannot answer that question inside an ED contract. SCEA at Yale is the exception because it binds nothing.
